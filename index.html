Situation 1:Hurricane Otis Alters Acapulco, Mexico
On Oct. 25, 2023, Hurricane Otis knocked over power lines, uprooted trees, and unleashed torrential flooding and landslides as it made landfall in Acapulco, Mexico, as one of the strongest storms in recorded history to hit the area. Acapulco, home to nearly 1 million people, is at the foot of steep mountains and surrounded by green tropical forests. Over a week after the category 5 storm made landfall, sediment runoff from excess rainfall that turned surrounding vegetation brown can still be seen.

Situation 2:Breakup of Sea Ice in the Amundsen Gulf, Canada
Each spring, sea ice from the winter months breaks apart and melts as temperatures start getting warmer in the Amundsen Gulf in northern Canada. In May 2023, the sea ice in the eastern part of the gulf quickly broke up, leaving behind ice chunks. By August, the Amundsen Gulf is usually ice-free.

Situation 3:Snowy Southern California
A late-February storm blanketed parts of Southern California in snow, including much of the San Gabriel Mountains north of Los Angeles, as seen in a satellite image from February 26, 2023. The storm even brought 2 inches (51 millimeters) of snow to low-elevation areas such as La Crescenta, a suburban community north of Los Angeles that sits at 2,000 feet (610 meters)
